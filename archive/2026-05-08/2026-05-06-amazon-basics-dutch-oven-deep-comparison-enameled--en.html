What's Actually Different: Enameled vs Pre-Seasoned

Enameled Cast Iron

The enamel coating is essentially glass fused onto the cast iron. No seasoning, no special care required—just wash with hot water after use.

Pros:

  • Won't rust (enamel completely seals the iron from air)
  • No maintenance needed, beginner-friendly
  • Multiple color options (blue, white, green, gray)

Cons:

  • Chipping is possible (exposed iron will rust)
  • Slightly slower heat transfer than bare cast iron
  • Higher price point

Pre-Seasoned Cast Iron

Factory-oiled and baked. Gets better with every use, but requires periodic maintenance.

Pros:

  • Fastest, most even heat transfer
  • Metal utensils welcome (even steel wool)
  • Lowest price point

-理论上寿命最长（可以传代）

Cons:

  • Requires adjustment period (hot water wash only, no soap)
  • Only available in black
  • May develop light rust if stored wet
