Coverage: Does the Spec Sheet Tell the Truth?

Large room model (B0DT63BVN3): Claims 2600sqft, CADR 580

In a 35㎡ living room with open kitchen (total ~45㎡), the large model on medium speed (speed 2 of 3) brought the air quality indicator from "yellow" to "green" in about 25 minutes. On max speed it was faster (~15 minutes) but noticeably louder.

Small room model (B0CL993435): Claims 1067sqft, CADR 238

In a 15㎡ bedroom, the small model performed well. On low speed it kept the room consistently in the green zone. On max speed it was slightly louder than I'd want for nighttime, but acceptable.

The math check: Standard formula is room area (sqft) × 2 = needed CADR for one air change per hour. For a 1000sqft room you want CADR ~200+. The small room model barely clears this for 1000sqft rooms - it's not oversized marketing, it's the actual rating.

For Chinese apartment sizes (divide sqft by 10.76): 1067sqft ≈ 99㎡, 2600sqft ≈ 241㎡

The large model has more total airflow so even on low it moves more air than the small model on its low - which is actually good, it means the large model can quietly maintain air quality whereas the small model needs to work harder.

Filter Replacement: The Hidden Cost

This is where many buyers get surprised:

  • **Large room model filter**: Amazon Basics replacement filter ~$25-30, recommended change every 6-12 months
  • **Small room model filter**: Amazon Basics replacement filter ~$18-25, same replacement interval

Real-world filter life: In a dusty city or with pets, filters last closer to 6 months. In clean areas, 12 months is realistic. Either way, budget $20-30/year for each purifier you own.

Tip: Run purifiers on lower speeds when possible - extends filter life significantly. The large model at 30% speed uses less energy and wears the filter slower than running the small model at 80% speed trying to cover the same area.

Which Model for Which Room

Buy the Large Room Model if:

  • Your main living area is 25㎡ or larger
  • Open floor plan (kitchen + living room combined)
  • You have pets (higher particulate load)
  • Allergies or asthma in the household
  • You want to be able to run it on lower speeds and still get good coverage

Buy: Amazon Basics Large Room Air Purifier (2600sqft, CADR 580)

Buy the Small Room Model if:

  • Bedroom or office is 10-20㎡
  • Budget-conscious but want real HEPA filtration
  • Renting (don't want to invest heavily in air quality)
  • Setting up a nursery or elderly person's room
  • First air purifier purchase (try the less expensive option first)

Buy: Amazon Basics Small Room Air Purifier (1067sqft, CADR 238)

Don't Buy Either if:

  • Your city has good outdoor air quality and natural ventilation works fine
  • You need formaldehyde removal (these are HEPA only, no activated carbon layer for VOCs)
  • You want to move the unit between rooms frequently - both are fairly heavy (large model ~12kg, small ~7kg)

---

The Practical Takeaway

There's no "better" model - there's only the right model for your space:

1. Get the large model if your main room is 25㎡+. The extra CADR means it won't strain to maintain air quality, runs quieter at any given speed, and covers truly open layouts.

2. Get the small model if you're filtering a single bedroom or office. At $79.99 it's the most cost-effective way to get real HEPA filtration in a smaller space without paying for overkill coverage.

3. Don't double up on small models to cover a large room - two small units at full speed will cost more in electricity and filter replacements than one properly-sized large unit at medium speed.
