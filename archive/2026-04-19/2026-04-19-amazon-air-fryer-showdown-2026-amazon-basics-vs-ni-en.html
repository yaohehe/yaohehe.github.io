Amazon Basics 3.2 Qt: Best for 1-2 people. I fit 6-8 chicken wings comfortably. More than that and they start crowding, which affects crispness. Suitable for individuals or couples.

Ninja 4L: The clear winner for families of 3-4. I fit 12 chicken wings in one batch. It even handled a whole 2.5lb chicken – something the other two simply can't accommodate due to space constraints.

Cosori 3.5L: The middle ground. Perfect for 2-3 person households. I fit 8-10 chicken wings without issues.

Bottom line: Choose Ninja for larger families. Amazon Basics is fine for singletons or couples who don't batch-cook.

Common Misconceptions Debunked

❌ Myth 1: Bigger capacity is always better

Reality: Larger units take longer to preheat and may not cook small portions evenly. If you're cooking for 1-2 people most of the time, a bigger fryer is overkill.

❌ Myth 2: Higher wattage always wins

Reality: More power means faster heating but also more noise and higher electricity draw. 1400W-1550W is the sweet spot for home air fryers – efficient without being disruptive.

❌ Myth 3: You don't need to flip food

Reality: Even with good airflow (especially in the Ninja), flipping large items like whole chickens halfway through produces noticeably more even browning.
