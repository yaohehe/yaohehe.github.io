Before You Buy: Ask Yourself These Three Questions

After two weeks of running all three Amazon Basics toaster models side by side in my kitchen, I'm going to save you the reading: there is no objectively "best" toaster — only the right one for your situation.

Start with these three quick questions:

1. Do more than two people need toast in the morning? If yes, the 2-slice will drive you insane with second-round waits.

2. Do you already own an air fryer? If you do, paying $80 for duplication doesn't make sense.

3. How much counter space do you actually have? The Air Fryer Oven is roughly the size of a compact microwave.

$21 Tier: Amazon Basics 2-Slice Toaster

Buy on Amazon

Who It's For

  • Singles or couples
  • Tiny kitchens with minimal counter space
  • Budget-constrained buyers needing basic toast function

Pros

Unbeatable value at $21. You're getting a 6-shade toaster from a recognized brand for the price of a decent coffee. The toast quality is solid — shade 4 produces results on par with most diner-style breakfast spots. The dial resistance feels precise, not cheap.

Compact footprint. About 28cm wide, 18cm tall. Fits in any corner without hogging space.

Defrost function works well. Frozen bread straight from the freezer to the defrost setting heats through evenly without burning the outside.

Cons

Two people means two rounds. This is the real pain point — each round takes 3-4 extra minutes. In a morning rush, you're watching the clock.

Only vertical slots. Bagels need to go in sideways or they get stuck. Getting a half-bagel to sit right is fidgety.

Crumb tray doesn't lock in place. When you pull it out, the panel loosens slightly. Over time this could let crumbs escape.

Bottom Line

Not bad, but not for a primary household machine. Great as a second toaster or for solo dwellers.

---

$35 Tier: Amazon Basics 4-Slice Toaster

Buy on Amazon

Who It's For

  • Families of three or more
  • Households in morning time crunch
  • People who occasionally toast non-standard items like bagels and croissants

Pros

Four slices at once, all done together. The 1600W power (nearly double the 2-slice) heats faster. All four slices come out with consistent color — better than I expected at this price.

Convection heating is the real differentiator. Few competitors at this price point offer convection. Turning it on for bagels means the bottom gets evenly toasted too, not just the cut surface.

Dual independent controls. Left and right sides can be set to different shades — white bread on the left at shade 4, whole wheat on the right at shade 3. This sounds minor but gets used almost daily in my household.

Cons

Bigger footprint. About 30cm wide, 20cm tall. Slightly wider than the 2-slice — measure your counter first.

1600W draws more power. Your electricity bill will be a few dollars higher per month compared to the 900W model. Small but real.

Same crumb tray looseness issue as the 2-slice.

Bottom Line

This is the model I'd recommend for most families. At $35, you get four key features without meaningful compromise. If you're unsure, buy this one.

---

$80 Tier: Amazon Basics 6-Slice Toaster Oven Air Fryer

Buy on Amazon

Who It's For

  • Kitchens without an air fryer
  • Small spaces that can't accommodate separate appliances
  • People who regularly roast large portions (whole chicken, 12" pizza, big vegetable trays)

Pros

Genuine multi-functionality. Air fry, bake, toast, roast, defrost, keep warm — one machine covers six use cases. 23QT capacity fits a whole chicken or a 12-inch pizza, which is solid for a compact oven of this size.

Air frying performance is surprisingly good. Fries, chicken nuggets, onion rings — no oil needed and they come out crispy. Oil absorption is 70-80% lower than deep frying, meaningful if you're cooking for a health-conscious household.

Temperature control is accurate. Has preset menus and custom temperature/time settings. In oven mode, temperature deviation is about ±10°F — more stable than most entry-level toaster ovens.

Double-glass door. Rare at this price point. Good insulation means the outer shell doesn't get dangerously hot during use.

Cons

Largest footprint by far. About 40cm wide, 30cm tall — comparable to a compact microwave. Renters with small kitchens need to measure before buying.

Jack of all trades, master of none. Air frying isn't quite as good as a dedicated air fryer (airflow is slightly weaker). Oven performance isn't quite as good as a dedicated compact oven (temperature limits and uniformity). This is the inherent trade-off of any multi-function appliance.

Harder to clean than a standard toaster. Interior has exposed heating elements and a fan. Crumbs get into places that are tedious to wipe down.

$80 is real money. You could buy a decent dedicated air fryer AND a basic toaster for this price. If you know you need both, splitting the purchase might serve you better.

Bottom Line

Makes sense if you're starting a kitchen from scratch and need one machine to do everything. But if you already have an air fryer or a separate oven, you're paying $80 for redundancy.
