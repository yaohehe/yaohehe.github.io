Plastic: The Budget-Friendly Workhorse

Amazon Basics plastic containers use BPA-free food-grade plastic — safety isn't a concern. The 10-piece set's size variety is genuinely useful, covering most daily needs.

Pros:

  • Lightweight — easy to carry around
  • 10-piece set offers flexible size combinations
  • Great value for the price
  • Lids seal well — no leaking

Cons:

  • Starts yellowing after ~3 months (especially with tomato sauce, curry)
  • Cannot go in the oven — limits usability
  • Edges show wear over time
  • Oil stains harder to clean than glass

---

Glass: The Long-Term Investment

The glass option's biggest advantage is versatility — it handles anything without worry. Tempered glass can go directly from fridge to oven, something plastic simply can't do. Marinate, bake, store — one container for the whole workflow.

Pros:

  • Never yellows, never absorbs odors
  • Oven/microwave safe — heat without transferring to another dish
  • Easy to clean, doesn't retain oil
  • Looks better — easy to see what's inside

Cons:

  • Heavy — noticeably heavier when filled
  • Only 3 pieces in the set — may need to buy more
  • Breaks if dropped (though mine survived 3 floor drops during testing)
  • Costs 50%+ more than plastic

Real Use Case Analysis

Plastic works better for:

  • Packing lunch for work — lightweight, no added burden
  • Storing fruits and snacks —不怕磕碰
  • Fridge organization — flexible 10-piece combinations
  • Baby food storage — safe, light, practical

Glass works better for:

  • Oven/microwave cooking — no dish transfer needed
  • Long-term food storage — no yellowing or odor absorption
  • Marinating meat — good seal, no seepage
  • Kitchen aesthetics — glass looks better on the counter

My Take

These two serve different purposes — there's no absolute winner. If you can only get one set:

  • Plastic 10-piece — higher flexibility, sufficient for everyday use
  • Glass 3-piece — long-term thinking, better for heating

The ideal setup is both: glass for food that needs reheating, plastic for everyday storage. This gives you the best of both worlds without overspending.
